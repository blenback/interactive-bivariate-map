Performance vs. Variation

How to interpret

Performance is calculated on a per-pixel basis as the mean of the cumulative sum of the change in ES and/or BD over all future time points for all scenarios. The use of the cumulative sum captures both positive and negative changes, thus a high value of Performance indicates that pixels generally display positive changes in ES and/or BD across all scenarios.

Stability is calculated using the Undesirable Deviation Metric which is the sum of the deviation (regret) away from the median value of the cumulative sum of the change in ES and/or BD for all instances below the median. In simple terms Stability captures the extent of negative variations in ES and/or BD across the scenarios, a higher value of stability indicates less negative variation with low values indicating more negative variation.
